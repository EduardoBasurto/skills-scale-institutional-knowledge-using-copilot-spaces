# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on agile practices. They partner with Project Managers to keep delivery running smoothly and protect the team's focus.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that slow team progress
- Coach the team on agile principles and continuous improvement
- Shield the team from unplanned interruptions and scope creep
- Track team velocity and help forecast capacity

### Goals
- Keep the team focused and delivery cadence consistent
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure ceremonies are effective and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint retrospectives
- Impediment logs and escalation to Project Managers
- Agile metrics summaries shared with leadership

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Removes technical blockers and helps maintain sustainable pace
- **Product Managers:** Coordinates backlog readiness and sprint goal alignment
- **Project Managers:** Partners on risk escalation, status reporting, and delivery coordination

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers represent the user's perspective from planning through release. They create research insights, wireframes, and prototypes that guide development toward usable, accessible, and delightful experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and accessibility reviews
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user stories and acceptance criteria
- Review implemented features to ensure design fidelity
- Advocate for user needs during trade-off discussions

### Goals
- Ensure delivered features are usable, accessible, and aligned with user needs
- Reduce rework caused by poor usability discovered late in the cycle
- Maintain a consistent and coherent product experience

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and usability test readouts
- Annotated wireframes and Figma/design file links in tickets
- Sync sessions with Developers and Product Managers during iteration planning

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Provides design specs, answers implementation questions, and reviews UI quality
- **Product Managers:** Co-creates user stories and validates that features meet user needs
- **Project Managers:** Flags design dependencies that may affect timelines or scope

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure automation, and deployment processes that enable teams to ship reliably and frequently.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and configuration as code
- Monitor system health, reliability, and performance
- Automate deployment, rollback, and environment provisioning
- Collaborate with Developers on build tooling and release processes

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize deployment risk and mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Maintain infrastructure reliability and cost efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Pipeline and deployment status dashboards and alerts
- Post-incident reviews and runbooks
- Release coordination with Developers and Project Managers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Supports build tooling, local environment parity, and deployment readiness
- **Product Managers:** Communicates release readiness and infrastructure constraints
- **Project Managers:** Coordinates deployment windows, change management, and rollback plans

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads advise on security risks, ensure secure coding and compliance standards are met, participate in threat modeling, and lead incident response activities.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct threat modeling and security architecture reviews
- Define and enforce secure coding standards and compliance policies
- Review code changes and infrastructure configurations for security vulnerabilities
- Lead security incident response and post-incident analysis
- Track security findings and drive remediation with development teams

### Goals
- Reduce the attack surface and eliminate critical vulnerabilities before release
- Ensure compliance with applicable regulations and organizational policies
- Build a security-conscious engineering culture

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings documented in tickets or pull request comments
- Risk advisories shared with Project Managers and leadership
- Incident response war-room communications and post-mortems

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Reviews code for vulnerabilities, advises on secure implementation patterns
- **Product Managers:** Raises security risks that may affect scope or timelines
- **Project Managers:** Escalates compliance blockers and coordinates security remediation sprints

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and refine requirements, translate business needs into actionable backlog items, and ensure development teams have the clarity needed to deliver the right solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements through stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Translate requirements into user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flows
- Refine and prioritize backlog items in collaboration with Product Managers
- Validate delivered features against original business requirements
- Identify process gaps and propose improvements

### Goals
- Ensure development work is grounded in well-understood, validated requirements
- Reduce ambiguity and rework caused by unclear or missing requirements
- Bridge communication between business stakeholders and the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents, process diagrams, and user story maps
- Backlog refinement sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Stakeholder update meetings and acceptance walkthrough sessions

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Clarifies requirements during sprint execution and answers implementation questions
- **Product Managers:** Co-owns backlog refinement and prioritization based on business value
- **Project Managers:** Surfaces requirement dependencies and risks that may affect delivery plans
